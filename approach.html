Our Approach

Test. Train. Track. Adapt.

Every athlete who joins Proformance starts with a baseline. We measure where you are, build a programme around it, then re-test on a cycle so progress is in the numbers, not the noise. Sports science isn't a marketing line at Proformance — it's the operating system.

The four sections below set out how we work: the testing battery, the movement framework, how we programme around competition, and the long-term arc that ties it all together.

03. Programming around the playing week

For competing athletes, the gym serves the sport — not the other way round. We build the week around match day. When the calendar allows we push strength; when it doesn't, we use match day primers to keep the athlete sharp without leaving anything on the gym floor.

Strength days (Tue/Wed) sit furthest from match. MD-1 primer is a short, sharp session: maintain neural drive without inducing fatigue. MD+1 is restorative — flush the system, address anything that came out of the game. Match day governs the week. If fixture density changes, the programme changes with it.

04. Long-term athlete development

The numbers above are a snapshot. Real development happens over years, not weeks. We work with athletes through growth phases, into senior performance, and beyond. The principle is straightforward: get the right work in at the right time, then keep showing up.

Stage 1 · Build
Movement quality first
Younger and developing athletes work on the movement patterns before they're loaded heavily. We're not chasing numbers at this stage — we're building the chassis. Growth-related setbacks are normal and planned around.
Stage 2 · Load
Strength becomes the priority
Once movement quality is in place, the loading goes up. This is where strength, power and speed metrics start moving meaningfully. Periodisation gets more deliberate. Testing cadence picks up.
Stage 3 · Compete
Programme serves performance
Senior competitive athletes work the gym around their playing week. Strength is maintained, peak performance is protected, and we use longitudinal data to spot what needs sharpening before it becomes a problem.
